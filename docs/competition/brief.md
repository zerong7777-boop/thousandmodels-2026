## Task Framing

This is a long-running AI Agent application competition rather than a single coding task. The project needs registration, concept design, proposal writing, prototype development, evaluation/demo preparation, practice article writing, presentation, and post-task knowledge capture.

## Participant Eligibility

The user states they satisfy the participant requirement for University of Macau 2026/2027 registered undergraduate/master students. Treat eligibility as satisfied, but still verify which proof document the registration form requires.

## Official Schedule

- Registration: 2026-05-08 to 2026-05-31.
- Briefing: 2026-05-18.
- Concept plan preparation/submission window: 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-21.
- Preliminary stage: 2026-07-01 to 2026-08-09.
- Semi-final stage: 2026-08-17 to 2026-08-31.
- Final roadshow: early September 2026.

## Competition Direction

The practical theme is Smart Macau and AI for a better life. The current working scope should focus on Macau tourism/culture, old district revitalization, elder-friendly services, public service, or a related locally grounded scenario.

## Judging Rubric

- Task completion: 30%.
- Agent capability: 20%.
- Application innovation: 20%.
- Practice article: 20%.
- Live presentation: 10%.

## Current Strategy

Prioritize a QwenPaw-based software Agent route first. This route has lower operational risk than the AI glasses route because it can be developed, tested, and demonstrated without depending on hardware access.
